Long Paper

SciTeller: An LLM-Based Framework for Persona-Adaptive Scientific Storytelling

A two-stage framework that transforms full scientific papers into narratives tailored to specific audience personas

EURECOM · Politecnico di Torino — Andrea Sillano, Pasquale Lisena, Raphaël Troncy, Tommaso Calò, Luigi De Russis

Lead developer — dataset construction, framework design, model fine-tuning, evaluation, and web application

SciTeller separates content planning from narrative realization through two components, Splitter and Storyteller, to generate scientific stories that adapt tone, depth, and emphasis to different audiences. The framework is grounded on a curated dataset of 62 scientific papers and 190 human-written stories, with persona annotations and section-level alignments used to supervise both outline planning and segment-level generation.

Research Focus

Most scientific communication systems still produce generic summaries with limited control over audience adaptation. This project studies how long scientific papers can be reorganized into faithful, coherent, and persona-aware narratives without relying on one-shot document generation.

Methods

  • Two-stage pipeline: Splitter + Storyteller
  • 62 papers, 190 stories, 8 personas, 496 outlines, 4090 story segments
  • Qwen2.5-7B full fine-tuning for planning and Qwen2.5-32B QLoRA for story generation
  • Semantic alignment between story paragraphs and paper sections
  • StoryScore evaluation with grounding, semantic alignment, hallucination, and redundancy signals

Outputs

  • SciTeller framework for persona-adaptive scientific storytelling
  • Zenodo dataset with papers, outlines, and story segments
  • Interactive web platform for upload, generation, editing, and export
  • Long paper submitted to LLM4Good @ ACM UMAP Workshops 2026

Research Report

Cinematic Speech as a Stress Test for Facial Animation Systems

An NLP and computer vision study of emotional bias in speech-driven facial animation benchmarks

EURECOM · Politecnico di Torino

Sole researcher — corpus curation, emotion classification, landmark extraction, analysis

This project investigates whether the standard benchmark used in speech-driven facial animation, VOCASET, reflects the emotional complexity of real narrative speech. The study compares VOCASET with a manually curated Disney Cinematic Corpus of 73 dialogues from seven Disney and Pixar films, then extends the analysis to facial animation outputs through landmark-based expressiveness measurements.

Research Focus

VOCASET is optimized for phonetic coverage rather than emotional diversity, introducing a structural bias toward neutral speech. The project asks whether cinematic dialogue can serve as a stress test for both emotion analysis models and facial animation systems trained on emotionally limited data.

Methods

  • Deduplicated VOCASET analysis compared against a balanced sample of 73 cinematic dialogues
  • Disney Cinematic Corpus built from 7 Disney and Pixar films
  • Emotion classification with DistilRoBERTa and manual fine-grained annotation
  • Human-model agreement analysis and per-category distribution gap
  • MediaPipe landmark extraction on speech-driven facial animation outputs

Outputs

  • Disney Cinematic Corpus with 73 annotated dialogues from 7 films
  • Evidence of a strong neutral-speech bias in VOCASET
  • 31.5% human-model agreement on cinematic dialogue
  • Research report connecting NLP analysis and facial expressiveness evaluation
